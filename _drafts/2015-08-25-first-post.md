## Trying out github

I have decided to learn the ways of git and make some of my work publicly available in [github](https://github.com/). You can check with I'm publicly developing at [my page](https://github.com/victor-navarro). Now it is pretty much empty, as I'm using it to host this blog files and make a repository for a simulator I developed a while ago.

## The Common-elements model of object categorization learning

What a long name. This model was developed by a fellow Chilean student, [Fabian Soto](http://people.psych.ucsb.edu/soto/fabian/) and [Edward Wasserman](http://psychology.uiowa.edu/people/edward-wasserman) a few years ago, in order to model how conceptualization with natural categories is acquired and expressed.

I developed the simulator in R, as it gave me the opportunity of learning the basics of that programming language and make something accessible for more users (you can download and hack your version of R, [freely](https://cran.r-project.org)).

The simulator code is rather ugly (I'm a novice programmer), and lacks a GUI, but it can be used to simulate simple experiments and has a fair share of customizability. I will probably keep developing it as different needs arise. Feel free to use it, fork it, and contribute as much as you want, I'll be glad to collaborate.

Oh, and by the way, you can download the paper [here](http://people.psych.ucsb.edu/soto/fabian/docs/PR.10.sw.pdf).
